Quick answer — pricing at a glance

If you need a fast answer: a **basic single-page meme coin site** ranges $150–$300; a **professional multi-page site** with live charts and better copy ranges $300–$600; complex builds (staking, minting, swap UI, or custom backend) start at **$1,000+** and can go much higher depending on integrations and security audits.

These are realistic freelance prices (developer + small contractor budget for art / copy). Agencies and full-stack teams usually charge more.

Package breakdown — What each price tier includes

Basic — Single-page Meme Coin Landing ($150–$300)

Best for: social-driven launches and meme coins where speed and minimal cost matter.

  • Custom single-page design (hero + About + Tokenomics + Roadmap + How to Buy)
  • Mobile-first responsive layout and basic accessibility
  • Simple contact form (Web3Forms or email) and social links
  • Deployment to Cloudflare Pages / GitHub Pages (static)
  • Performance optimizations: compressed images (webp), minified CSS/JS
  • Basic on-page SEO: meta title, description, canonical, OG image

Why this price: One page, limited integrations, faster turnaround, minimal custom assets. Perfect when you just need a polished landing to support a social campaign.

Professional — Multi-page or Feature-rich Site ($300–$600)

Best for: longer-term projects, token presales, projects that want credibility and better discovery.

  • Multi-page site (Landing, Tokenomics, Docs/Whitepaper, Roadmap, Contact)
  • Live price chart integration (DexScreener / CoinGecko embed)
  • Improved UI/UX, animations, copy editing (basic)
  • Enhanced SEO structure: JSON-LD, sitemaps, structured headings, internal linking plan
  • Faster performance optimizations and lazy-loading images
  • Staging preview and one round of client revisions

Why this price: More pages, more copy, better SEO setup, and trust-building content — these increase conversion and organic traffic.

Custom DApp / Platform — Enterprise & Integrations ($1,000+)

Best for: projects requiring on-chain interactions, wallets, staking, NFT mints, or a dashboard with back-end data.

  • WalletConnect, MetaMask & on-chain interactions
  • Custom UI for staking, swaps, or mint pages (with client-side safety checks)
  • Serverless backend functions (if required) for forms, webhooks, or analytics
  • Security hardening and optional audit coordination (third-party)
  • Full content strategy and analytics setup (GA4, events, email capture flows)

Why this price: More complexity, testing, and security responsibility. Expect longer timelines and higher QA costs.

Hidden costs & recurring fees you should budget for

Clients often forget recurring and third-party costs. These are real and can affect your monthly budget:

  • Domain registration: ~$10–$25/year (varies by TLD).
  • Hosting & bandwidth: Static sites on Cloudflare Pages are low-cost; dynamic features may require serverless functions or a VPS (costs vary $0–$20+/month).
  • Third-party APIs: Coin price APIs, analytics, or charts sometimes have paid tiers when traffic grows.
  • Payment processing: If you accept payments (for ecommerce or donations), payment gateway fees apply (Stripe/PayPal % per transaction).
  • Security & audits: Smart contract audits are expensive (thousands to tens of thousands) and separate from web development. Website security hardening and penetration testing also cost extra if required.
  • Content & copy: Professional copywriting, illustrations, or motion assets may be quote-addons.

Recommendation: Budget for at least one month of running costs and a small contingency for API limits or extra design work.

Integrations and features that raise cost — and why they matter

Wallet integrations: MetaMask & WalletConnect

Adding wallet connectivity requires careful implementation and extra testing. Mistakes here can break the user experience and create security risks.

Impact on cost: +$100–$400 depending on complexity.

On-chain interactions (staking, minting, swaps)

These require front-end UI + reliability checks and often require coordination with smart contract developers. Testing and sandboxing add time.

Impact on cost: +$300–$2000 depending on features.

Live data & charts

Embedding charts (DEX Screener, CoinGecko) is usually straightforward, but building custom live charts that query an API and render client-side is more complex.

Impact on cost: +$50–$400.

Analytics, tracking & conversion setup

Event tracking (GA4 + GTM + conversions) costs more time but pays off. Proper tracking shows what marketing channels convert.

Impact on cost: +$50–$250.

Security & audits — web vs smart contracts

Be clear: web development security (HTTPS, CSP, XSS prevention) is different from smart contract audits. Both matter, but costs differ hugely.

  • Website security: included as part of development (HTTPS, Content Security Policy, input sanitization). Minimal extra cost when planned.
  • Smart contract audits: external specialist services — often expensive (thousands to tens of thousands).

Advice: Do not skip basic web security: HTTPS, secure headers, correct CORS and limited client-side secret usage. For any on-chain logic, always plan for an audit budget.

Timeline — how long will it take?

Typical timelines (estimate):

  • Basic single page: 2–5 days (design → build → deploy)
  • Professional multi-page: 1–3 weeks (content, design, revisions)
  • Custom DApp: 4+ weeks (integration, testing & staging)

Timelines depend on client responsiveness (content, images), number of revisions, and complexity of integrations. A clear brief reduces time and cost.

Suggested milestone structure

  1. Discovery / brief (1–2 days)
  2. Design & wireframes (2–5 days)
  3. Development (2–14+ days depending on scope)
  4. Testing & staging (1–7 days)
  5. Go-live & post-launch checks (1–3 days)

SEO, performance & indexability — what to budget for

For token sites, SEO is a major long-term traffic driver. Money spent on SEO fundamentals returns better leads and investor searches.

On-page SEO essentials (included in Professional package)

  • Unique H1 per page & semantic headings (H1–H6 properly used)
  • Meta title & description tailored for primary keyword
  • Canonical tags, Open Graph and Twitter Card images
  • JSON-LD for Organization / Service / Article / FAQ when relevant
  • Optimized image `alt` attributes & responsive `srcset` (you told me to keep single image — still add good `alt` text)

Technical SEO & performance

  • Fast CDN hosting (Cloudflare Pages): reduces TTFB and global latency.
  • Minified CSS/JS, critical CSS for hero to reduce CLS.
  • Lazy-loading below-the-fold images
  • Structured internal linking that avoids keyword cannibalization (see internal linking notes below)

Tip: Use one primary keyword per page and avoid repeating exact-match anchors across the site. Link to your service pages with varied anchors like “crypto website services”, “token presale landing”, or “see token case studies”.

SEO & keyword cannibalization — how to avoid ranking fights

Common mistake: multiple pages target the same short keyword (e.g., “crypto website developer”). That makes Google unsure which page to show. Instead:

  • Pick one primary keyword per page. For example: Services (hub) = “web development services”, Crypto subpage = “crypto website development”.
  • Use varied anchor text when linking internally (avoid repeating exact-match anchors everywhere).
  • Make case studies and blog posts target long-tail queries (e.g., “how much does a crypto website cost”, “meme coin launch checklist 2026”).

Follow the internal linking and keyword map I shared earlier: it prevents cannibalization and helps the right page rank for the right intent.

Which metrics matter after launch?

  • Organic sessions: Are people finding the site via Google?
  • Bounce rate & time on page: Is the tokenomics page engaging visitors?
  • Conversion rate: How many visitors sign up to the presale / contact form?
  • Mobile performance: For crypto projects, many users come from phones — check Core Web Vitals on mobile.

Set up GA4 events for CTA clicks, presale signups, and important form submits. I include basic event tracking in Professional packages.

Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

Q: Can you build and host the site on Cloudflare Pages?

A: Yes — Cloudflare Pages + GitHub is my preferred static deployment for speed and global performance. For serverless functions or back-end tasks, I recommend Cloudflare Workers or a small VPS depending on needs.

Q: Will a basic site rank on Google?

A: A basic single-page site alone rarely ranks top for competitive queries. For discoverability you need: unique content, backlinks, and a content strategy (blog/case studies). Use the professional package and a blog to target long-tail keywords.

Q: Do you do smart contract audits?

A: I do not perform smart contract audits myself. I can coordinate with reputable audit firms and prepare the web/app for integration once the contract is audited.

Q: How fast can we launch?

A: With required assets & copy ready, a basic site can launch within a few days. Multi-page or custom projects need more planning — I’ll give an accurate timeline on discovery.
